Lease assignment step by step - a complete guide

In today's dynamic business environment, entrepreneurs often face the need to adjust their financial obligations. One solution that's gaining popularity is lease assignment. In this article, we'll discuss in detail what a lease assignment is, how it works, and what to pay special attention to throughout the process.

What is a lease assignment?

Lease assignment is the legal transfer of rights and obligations under a leasing agreement from the current lessee to the new lessee. In other words, it's a way to "transfer" your lease to another person or company, who will assume all obligations related to the agreement. This is a legal solution, but it must meet certain conditions and require the leasing company's consent.

When should you consider assigning a lease?

Lease assignment may be a beneficial solution in the following situations:

  • When an entrepreneur wants to withdraw from their business
  • In case of financial problems and difficulties in paying installments
  • When the leased asset is no longer needed by the company
  • When reorganizing a company or changing its business profile
  • In a situation where we found a better financing offer

Lease assignment process - step by step

1. Verification of the possibility of assignment

The first step is to check whether your leasing agreement allows for assignment. Carefully review the terms of the agreement or contact the leasing company directly.

2. Finding a taker

The next step is to find a person or company interested in taking over the lease. The transferee must meet the criteria set by the leasing company, including adequate creditworthiness.

3. Submitting an application

Once you've identified a potential transferee, you'll need to submit a transfer request to the leasing company. Required documents typically include:

  • Application for consent to the assignment
  • Financial documents of the acquirer
  • Company registration documents (in the case of entrepreneurs)
  • Certificates of no arrears with ZUS and US

What should you pay special attention to?

Assignment costs

Before starting the process, you should carefully analyze all costs associated with the assignment:

  • Application processing fee
  • Fee for carrying out the assignment
  • Notary fees (if required)
  • Possible costs of valuation of the leased asset

Summary

Lease assignment can be an effective solution in situations where we want or need to terminate a lease. The key to success is a thorough analysis of all aspects of the transaction, careful preparation of documentation, and selection of a reliable partner to assume the obligations.

Remember that if you have any doubts, it is worth consulting a financial advisor or lawyer who will help you conduct the entire process safely and legally.
